About the Project

The KAUST Reefscape Restoration Initiative is a large-scale coral reef restoration program in the Red Sea in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia (KSA), begun in 2021 and funded by King Abdullah University of Science and Technology (KAUST) , a world-class graduate research university in Thuwal, Saudi Arabia on the Red Sea north of Jeddah, in partnership with NEOM , a region in northwest Saudi Arabia that is being sustainably developed from the ground up with the world’s most advanced technologies to redefine how people live, work, and play.

The initiative will begin with a 100-hectare reef restoration project at Shushah Island. This will be a global demonstration of coral restoration that integrates innovative, in-Kingdom and internationally developed technologies, pioneering propagation, planting and monitoring approaches, and international expertise to accelerate solutions for reef ecosystems at a time of unprecedented environmental change. KAUST’s Coral Nursery in NEOM:

KAUST will design, construct and operate the world's largest and most technologically advanced coral nursery at Haddah beach in NEOM with expected production of up to 400,000 corals per year. This nursery will be a long-term facility with infrastructure including life support systems and will employ advanced techniques for coral propagation at scale required to support the enhancement and restoration of the reefscape at Shushah and beyond.

Shushah Island Reefscape:

KAUST will prepare, conserve, enhance, monitor and manage ~100 hectares of reefscape near Shushah Island. This will be done by dividing it up into operational grids for master planning purposes. Each grid's current condition will be mapped and assessed in detail. Master planning for the entire site will be done first and then a strategy will be developed for each grid. Some grids will have significant structural augmentation (placement of limestone reef boulders and/or coral pods) and a high density of coral outplants.

Digital Twin and Monitoring:

Using a suite of sensors and advanced data visualization tools, KAUST will track the performance and health of corals at different stages of growth, and adjust strategies as needed for optimal conditions. As part of the program, a digital twin of the reefscape will be created which will allow superlative, non-invasive educational, interactive digital experiences that will engage tourists, scientists and students around the world in marine conservation and Red Sea coral reefscapes.

Pilot Nursery:

We are creating an integrated reef restoration model, with the world’s largest and most advanced land-based coral nursery. A 1000 sq.m pilot facility, the KAUST Coral Nursery will test and trial techniques and technologies, optimize operational plans and train staff so that marine and dive operations are executed effectively once commissioned.

KAUST Shushah Island Research Center:

The research center, to be built at Shushah Island, will engage researchers and scientists-in-residence from the Kingdom and around the world with a state-of-the-art research facility to advance knowledge about corals and reefscape ecosystems. The facility will also support the reefscape program with monitoring and visualization efforts and will act as the base for the program’s dive operations.
